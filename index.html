Portfolio VaR & Risk Metrics

Build a portfolio (up to 5 securities) and calculate Value at Risk (VaR) using different methods:

  • Normal VaR – Assumes normal distribution of returns.
  • Historical VaR – Uses actual past returns.
  • Monte Carlo VaR – Simulates future returns.
  • Cornish-Fisher VaR – Adjusts for skewness and kurtosis.

Note: VaR calculations depend on historical price data availability.
